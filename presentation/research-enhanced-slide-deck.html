üß† Evidence-Based Design Principles

Six Core Principles from Learning Science

  1. Anchor Before Apply: Build robust mental schemas before contextual application (Schema Theory)
  2. Progressive Guidance Fading: High scaffolding ‚Üí medium ‚Üí minimal support (Cognitive Apprenticeship)
  3. Load-Aware Materials: Minimize extraneous load, optimize germane processing (Cognitive Load Theory)
  4. Retrieval and Spacing: Distributed practice with spaced repetition (Testing Effect)
  5. Information Triage: Teach filtering strategies and attention management (Attention Research)
  6. Constructive Alignment: Outcomes, activities, and assessments aligned (Biggs & Tang)

Measurement Framework

  • Paas Scale: 1-9 mental effort rating for real-time load monitoring
  • Leppink Multidimensional: Separate tracking of intrinsic, extraneous, and germane load
  • Transfer Assessment: Near, moderate, and far transfer evaluation rubrics

üìÖ Research-Validated Course Flow

12-Week Progressive Implementation

  1. Weeks 1-2: Pivot Intensive - Core concept mastery, diagnostic assessment, retrieval practice initiation
  2. Weeks 3-5: Launch A (Near Transfer) - High scaffolding, same domain application, peer teaching integration
  3. Weeks 6-8: Launch B (Moderate Transfer) - New contexts, scaffolding reduction, information triage emphasis
  4. Weeks 9-11: Launch C (Far Transfer) - Cross-domain challenges, minimal scaffolding, complex problem solving
  5. Week 12: Synthesis - Cumulative assessment, portfolio reflection, transfer evidence documentation

Research Validation

This progression follows evidence from active learning meta-analyses (Freeman et al., 2014) and transfer optimization studies (Barnett & Ceci, 2002), ensuring maximum learning effectiveness while maintaining appropriate cognitive load levels.
